1) acquire more workshops 2) sell more products to workshops and 3) get their products out to end consumers.

The Objective

I went through the business vision and priorities with the product owner, to make sure we understand what benefits they want to see. I summarised wider Castrol market research to understand the personas better and the purchase behaviours and influencing factors for choosing Castrol as a brand. I also interviewed key Castrol stakeholders to get a list of existing Castrol products offered today to sales representatives and IWS.

Once I had a good understanding of the user landscape, I created the USA and China-based personas, and created an As-Is user journey for the USA, along with the user needs and pain points.

The final output was a high-level presentation of the user's drivers/motivations, needs, discomfort points, digital tools used by the user, and digital tools provided by Castrol. JOB ROLE BIAS DETECTOR

The Challenge

The subtle use of certain words in your role descriptions can subconsciously discourage minorities from applying for your role. On slack, we take full advantage of the ability to reach hundreds of IBMers at a time and post about role opportunities multiple times a day. But who is making these slack posts? Well, chances are they don't work in recruitment. They are business analysts, developers, designers, and the list goes on. So how can we instil best practices when it comes to creating accessible role descriptions that foster a diverse workforce?

My Role

My role was to look at the US and China market, and do research on the user groups, which were the sales representatives that work for distributors and sell Castrol products to workshops, IWS owners (the owners of the workshops) and the IWS mechanics.

The Objective

I did a synoptic project to analyse the current internal business environment relating to the applications process for professionals new to IBM, and how we can utilise technology to close the gap between the offer stage and the role acceptance stage in a manner that fosters diversity. I designed the entire application and built it myself, and did usability testing with IBMers. I coded the site using Python, HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I used IBM Watson to create a sentiment analysis of the job descriptions entered.

The Outcome

I created the Talent Acquisition Bias Detector; A simple web app that allows you to easily paste your job description in to check for any subconsciously biased language.

It also includes some tips for best practice, for example, If you include overly complex or dense language, you potentially exclude candidates who are perfectly capable of doing the job but struggle with reading or language comprehension.

The Feedback

"I think this is a great project that you have been working on as it is very valuable in helping not only IBM, but all businesses become more inclusive. This will also help others develop inclusive communications elsewhere if they are using this tool regularly. Looks like a fantastic piece of work, well done!"

- HANNAH BRAY

BBC SOUNDS

The Challenge

There are not enough 15-24 year olds listening to BBC sounds. Our team was tasked with designing a solution for this gap in the market.

My Role

My role was to design the experience for an app for BBC sounds that would appeal to 15-24 year olds.

The Objective

As a team, we wanted to design a more personalised journey for a user, where they would only get recomendations tailored to them. We also needed to design something that would improve the app's search functionality, and increase accessibility at the same time.

The Outcome

I designed the user experience for an app that uses Watson speech-to-text to create audio transcripts for each sound file. We can use these transcripts to create a better search functionality where sound bites from each audio file are included in search results. We also designed it to use artificial intelligence for more tailored recommendations for the user. I designed the screens to include some transparency as to why certain recommendations were made for a user. The final outcome was a set of high level wireframes presented to the client.
